International Congress of Philosophy and Technology

Place: Berlin, Germany

Date: September 2025

Philosophers, scientists, and ethics experts gathered to debate the future of artificial intelligence (AI). In an international symposium held this week at the University of Berlin, leaders in philosophy, science, and ethics analyzed the social, political, and moral implications of the growing role of AI in everyday life. The event brought together experts from various disciplines and countries, promoting interdisciplinary dialogue on the challenges and opportunities presented by this technology.
The importance of ethical education in the development of emerging technologies and the shared responsibility among governments, companies, and civil society to prevent potential abuses was also discussed.
The urgency of integrating ethics from the early stages of AI system design and programming was highlighted.
At the end, experts agreed on the need to continue promoting spaces for global dialogue and collaboration to ensure that AI contributes to social well-being without compromising fundamental human rights.

Discovery of Aristotle's Manuscripts

Place: Thessaloniki, Greece

Date: August 2025

A team of archaeologists found unpublished fragments of texts attributed to Aristotle, related to practical ethics.
During an excavation in the ancient city of Stagira, Aristotle's birthplace, several papyrus fragments were discovered bearing writings attributed to the philosopher.
These documents address topics of practical ethics, a central area in his work but little explored in the preserved texts. Philosophy and paleography experts highlight that the texts could contain previously unknown teachings on virtue, happiness (eudaimonia), and moral decision-making in everyday life. This discovery offers a unique opportunity to reassess Aristotelian ethics and its practical application.
